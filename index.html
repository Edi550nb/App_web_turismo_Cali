Historic Center

It is the historical heart and geographic center of Cali, Colombia. Located between 4th and 5th Avenues and 11th and 12th Streets, it is famous for its iconic, towering date palms and its neoclassical and republican architecture.

Officially known as the Church of Our Lady of Solitude, it is one of the most important architectural and religious landmarks in Cali, Colombia. Located on the banks of the Cali River, this neo-Gothic gem is a must-see for locals and tourists alike, thanks to its historical and visual beauty.

The Bulevar del Río (officially Bulevar de la Avenida Colombia) is the most iconic pedestrian walkway in Cali, Colombia. Inaugurated in 2013, this 980-meter-long space was built over the country's longest urban tunnel to revitalize the area adjacent to the Cali River and transform it into a cultural and tourist hub.

Traditional Food

Cholado (or cholao) is the signature dessert of Cali and the Valle del Cauca region. It consists of a refreshing mixture of shaved ice, sweet syrups, a variety of chopped fresh fruit, condensed milk, and optionally, a cookie or ice cream.

The chuleta valluna is one of the most emblematic dishes of the Valle del Cauca region of Colombia. It consists of a cut of pork (usually loin or leg) that is flattened very thinly, marinated with spices, and served breaded and fried.

The empanada is a cultural institution, characterized by its crispy corn dough and its filling of meat with potato.

Viewpoints

The Christ the King Monument is one of Cali's most iconic landmarks, located on Cerro de los Cristales (Hill of Crystals). Recently, the area was transformed into the Cristo Rey Ecopark, a comprehensive project offering ecological trails, viewpoints with 360° views of the city and the Farallones de Cali mountain range, and spaces dedicated to environmental conservation.

The Monument to Sebastián de Belalcázar is one of the most iconic symbols of Cali, Colombia. It is dedicated to the Spanish conquistador who founded the city in 1536.

The Cerro de las Tres Cruces (Hill of the Three Crosses) is one of Cali's most iconic natural landmarks and viewpoints, located at 1,480 meters above sea level in the Western Cordillera. It is a favorite destination for Cali residents to hike, play sports, and enjoy panoramic views of the entire city.

Festivals

The Cali Fair, also known as the Sugarcane Fair, is the most important cultural and festive event in Santiago de Cali, Colombia. It is celebrated annually from December 25th to 30th and is a symbol of Cali's identity, distinguished by its passion for salsa, joy, and diversity.

The Cali World Salsa Festival 2026 (formerly known as the International Salsa Festival) will be held from September 24 to 27, 2026. This event is considered the most important salsa gathering in the world, bringing together dancers, musicians, and music lovers in the World Capital of Salsa.

The Cali International Film Festival (FICCali) is the most important film event in southwestern Colombia. The most recent edition (the 17th) took place from October 21 to 26, 2025, under the theme Cinema to Connect."
