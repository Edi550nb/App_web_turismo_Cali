Ecoparks

Christ the Kinge

The Cristo Rey Ecopark is one of Cali's most emblematic tourist and religious destinations, recently renovated as part of a comprehensive project that seeks to connect the city with its guardian hills.

Biodiversity Ecopark COP16

The COP16 Biodiversity Ecopark (formerly known as Corazón de Pance) is a 92-hectare natural space located in southern Cali, designed as the main environmental legacy of the COP16 summit. This "green lung" seeks the conservation of species and environmental education through a sustainability model that uses solar energy and rainwater harvesting.

https://90minutos.co

ecopark lake of the herons

The Lago de las Garzas Ecopark is a wetland and natural refuge located in southern Cali, specifically in the Pance sector (Commune 22). It is an ideal space for contemplative ecotourism, relaxation, and environmental education without leaving the city.

Sights

Hill of the Three Crosses

The Hill of the Three Crosses is one of Cali's most iconic landmarks, located in the western mountain range northwest of the city. It's a favorite destination for Cali residents for sports, hiking, and religious tourism, offering one of the best panoramic views of the Branch of Heaven.

Image taken from:

https://90minutos.co/

Statue of Sebastian de Belalcazar

The Statue of Sebastián de Belalcázar is one of Cali's most iconic monuments, located on a viewpoint on a hill west of the city that offers a panoramic view of the capital of Valle.

Image taken from:

https://www.cali.gov.co/

The River Cat

The River Cat, popularly known as Tejada's Cat, is one of the most iconic sculptures in Cali, Colombia. It was created by the renowned Bogota artist Hernando Tejada and has become a symbol of Cali's identity since its inauguration in July 1996.

https://www.elpais.com.co/
