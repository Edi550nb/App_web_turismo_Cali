Historic Center

Cayzedo Square

Cayzedo Square or Caicedo Square is the historical heart and main landmark of Cali. Located between Carrera 4 and 5 and Calle 11 and 12, this space of approximately 12,000 square meters has been the epicenter of the city's social and political life since colonial times.

River Boulevard

The River Boulevard is one of the most emblematic urban spaces in Cali, Colombia. It is a 980-meter-long pedestrian corridor that runs along the Cali River, connecting the historic center with the north of the city.

Image taken from:

https://travel.valledelcauca.gov.co

Enrique Buenaventura Municipal Theatre

The Enrique Buenaventura Municipal Theater is the cultural epicenter of Cali, a national monument in the Italian classical style inaugurated in 1927. It is located in the historic center and is recognized for its excellent acoustics and its facade inspired by Venetian architecture.

Religious Sites

La Ermita Church

The Church of La Ermita, officially known as the Church of Our Lady of Solitude of the River, is the most iconic architectural symbol of Cali, Colombia. It is distinguished by its imposing neo-Gothic style and its strategic location facing the Cali River, in the heart of the River Boulevard

Image taken from:

https://caracol.com.co/

Church and Convent of La Merced

The Church of La Merced in Cali, Colombia, is one of the city's most emblematic colonial monuments, recognized as the site of the first mass held after the founding of Cali in 1536. It is considered the oldest building in the city that still retains elements from its founding period, such as its original roofs, doors, and water pipes.

Image taken from:

https://www.colombia.com/

Church of San Francisco

The Church of San Francisco in Cali is one of the city's most important architectural and religious gems. Located in the heart of the historic center, it forms part of a complex that represents the cultural memory of the people of Cali.
